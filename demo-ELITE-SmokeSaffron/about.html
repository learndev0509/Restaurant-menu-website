The beginning

A borrowed tandoor, big ambitions

In 2019, three siblings and a borrowed tandoor opened a twelve-seater on Ashram Road. Within a year, there was a waitlist.

We started with one belief: that great food is built on fire and patience. Slow-simmered dals. Hand-ground masalas. A tandoor lit fresh every day at 10 AM. The shortcuts most kitchens take, we refused — because every plate we send out is one we'd happily eat ourselves.

Six years on, the kitchen has grown, the bar has stayed open later, and the seats have multiplied. But the rhythm hasn't changed. Spice gets ground at dawn. The chef still tastes every batch of makhani himself. And the welcome is still warm enough to remember.

The journey

Six years, five chapters

2019

The first twelve seats

A 400 sq.ft kitchen, one tandoor, and a menu of twelve dishes. We opened in Diwali week — and the city showed up.

2021

The bar & the oven arrive

We added a late-night bar, a coffee counter, and a wood-fired pizza oven. The same kitchen, now open from noon to midnight.

2023

Private dining opens

A dedicated 40-seat private room for the celebrations our guests kept asking for — birthdays, anniversaries, corporate evenings.

2024

Ahmedabad Times pick

Featured among the city's most-loved late-night kitchens. We took the team out for biryani to celebrate.

2026

Where we are now

80+ dishes. A full bar. One belief, still: that food cooked over live fire, with patience, simply tastes different.

Meet the chef

"If you can't taste the fire in it, the dish failed. We taste everything before it leaves the kitchen."

Chef Rajesh trained in Delhi at the iconic Bukhara, spent four years in London with two Michelin-starred kitchens, and came back to Ahmedabad with a single goal — to cook the food his family cooked at home, only better.

The makhani is his grandmother's recipe. The biryani is from his uncle's wedding feast. The pasta is his own.

Chef Rajesh Patel
Founder & Executive Chef

What we believe

Four simple things

No fads. No shortcuts. Just the things that make food worth the trouble.

Fresh, daily

Spices ground at dawn, bread baked at ten, tandoor lit fresh. Nothing kept overnight.

Slow, on purpose

Dal makhani simmers overnight. Some things simply shouldn't be rushed.

Honest pricing

What you pay reflects what's on the plate — never inflated, never apologetic about quality.

Warm welcomes

First visit or hundredth, you're greeted like family — because that's how we'd want to be greeted.
