Our Story

From a family kitchen, with love

Six years. Three generations. One unwavering pursuit — flavour that feels like home.

Home / Our Story

The Beginning

A small kitchen, big ambitions

In 2019, three siblings and a borrowed tandoor opened a 12-seater on the corner of Sardar Patel Ring Road. Within a year, we had a waitlist.

We started with one belief: that a great Indian meal is built on patience. Slow-simmered dals. Hand-ground masalas. Tandoor that's lit fresh every day at 10 AM. The shortcuts most kitchens take, we refused — because every plate we send out is a plate someone in our family would happily eat.

Six years on, the kitchen has grown, the menu has expanded, and the seats have multiplied. But the rhythm hasn't changed. Spice gets ground at dawn. The chef still tastes every batch of makhani himself. And the welcome is still warm enough to remember.

The Journey

Six years, five chapters

2019

The first 12 seats

A 400 sq.ft kitchen, one tandoor, and a menu of twelve dishes. We opened in October — Diwali week — and the city showed up.

2021

The cafe arrives

We added a coffee bar, a small bakery, and a wood-fired pizza oven. The same kitchen, now open from breakfast to midnight.

2023

Private dining opens

A dedicated 40-seat private room, designed for the celebrations our guests had been quietly asking for — birthdays, anniversaries, corporate evenings.

2024

Ahmedabad Times pick

Featured as one of "Ahmedabad's 25 most-loved family restaurants." We took the team out for biryani to celebrate. The biryani was great. The biryani was theirs.

2026

Where we are now

80+ dishes. 250 guests a day. One belief, still: that food made with patience tastes different. That's the whole secret. There isn't another one.

Meet The Chef

A lifetime in the kitchen

"My grandmother used to say — if you can't taste the love in it, the recipe failed. We taste everything before it leaves the kitchen. Everything."

Chef Rajesh trained in Delhi at the iconic Bukhara, spent four years in London with two Michelin-starred kitchens, and came back to Ahmedabad with a single goal — to cook the food his family had cooked at home, only better. The makhani recipe is his grandmother's. The biryani is from his uncle's wedding feast. The pasta is his own.

What We Believe

Four simple things

No fads. No shortcuts. Just the four things we believe make good food worth the trouble.

Fresh, daily

Spices ground at dawn. Bread baked at 10. Tandoor lit fresh. Nothing kept overnight.

Slow, on purpose

Dal makhani is simmered overnight. Biryani is dum-cooked for 90 minutes. Some things shouldn't be rushed.

Honest pricing

What you pay reflects what's on the plate — never inflated, never apologetic about quality.

Warm welcomes

Whether it's your first visit or your hundredth, you're greeted like family — because that's how we'd want to be greeted.
